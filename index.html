What does the WebApp do?

This is a simple demonstration on random samples and confidence interval. http://junuylia.shinyapps.io/project/

You are given the choice of different distributions and you can generate a random sample. The application produces the histogram of the generated sample, and it also gives the confidence interval based on the chosen significance level. Confidence interval and the population mean are indicated both on the plot.

The help doc is available on http://junuylia.shinyapps.io/help

How does the WebApp work?

  1. Choose from the left panel the desired sample size. As this is a simple demo, we limit the sample size to between 50 and 1000.
  2. Choose from the distribution list.
    • Normal: $y\sim N(\mu,\sigma),$ where $\mu$ is the population mean and $\sigma$ is the standard deviation.
    • Poisson: $y\sim Pois(\lambda),$ where $\lambda$ is the population mean and $\sqrt\lambda$ is the standard deviation.
    • Binomial: $y\sim Bin(N,p),$ where $N$ is the number of trials and $p$ is the possibility of success. The population mean is calculated as $\mu=np$.
    • Uniform: $y\sim Unif(A,B),$ where $A$ and $B$ are lower and upper bound, and $\mu$ is the average of $A$ and $B$.
  3. Significance Level $\alpha$: This is the area on the tail of the distribution density. Note that confidence level is equal to $1-\alpha$.

An Example

Let's generate a random sample of 100 from normal distribution with \(\mu=5\) and \(\sigma=2\). We would like to find the \(95\%\) confidence interval of the population mean.

The significance level is obtained by

alpha = 1-0.95
